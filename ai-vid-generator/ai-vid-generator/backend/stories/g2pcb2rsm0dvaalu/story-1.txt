South African billionaire Johann Rupert has overtaken Nigerian industrialist Aliko Dangote as Africa’s richest person, according to the latest data from the Bloomberg Billionaires Index. Rupert controls Richemont, one of the world's largest luxury goods firms, which owns brands like Cartier and Montblanc. His net worth has surged by $1.9bn to $14.3bn, placing him at 147th position globally, 12 places ahead of Dangote. The Nigerian's fortune has fallen by $1.7bn this year, giving him a net worth of $13.4bn. The downturn in Dangote’s wealth underscores Nigeria’s challenging economic environment.
