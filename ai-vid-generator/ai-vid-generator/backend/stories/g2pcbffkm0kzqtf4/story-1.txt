Kilmartin Glen in Argyll, Scotland, is one of Britain's most important archaeological landscapes, yet it's largely unknown. This area is home to over 800 ancient relics, including henge monuments, burial cairns, standing stones, and stone circles. These structures predate the pyramids and Stonehenge. The glen's history spans over 5,000 years, and its archaeological significance continues to grow with ongoing discoveries. The recently revamped Kilmartin Museum showcases many of these finds, offering a glimpse into the prehistoric past. Despite its rich history, Kilmartin Glen remains a hidden gem waiting to be explored.
