Viche, a cultural treasure of Colombia, is gaining popularity as bartenders smuggle it internationally. This traditional liquor, made from sugarcane, is integral to Afro-Colombian rituals and celebrations. Despite its rich heritage, viche faces challenges from legal restrictions and cultural appropriation. Recent laws aim to protect its authenticity, but regulatory hurdles persist. As new brands emerge, the fight to preserve viche's cultural significance continues, ensuring that this ancestral drink remains true to its roots.
