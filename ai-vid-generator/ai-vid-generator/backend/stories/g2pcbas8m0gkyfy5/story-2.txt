Viche, a traditional Colombian liquor, is deeply rooted in Afro-Colombian culture, used in rituals and celebrations. Despite its rich history, it faces challenges from cultural appropriation and legal restrictions. As its popularity grows, bartenders are smuggling it internationally, highlighting its demand. Recent laws aim to protect viche's heritage, but regulatory hurdles remain. The fight to preserve its cultural significance continues as new brands emerge, risking the authenticity of this ancestral drink.
